Help us build.

Every dollar lands directly in students' hands — robot parts, registration fees, travel to competitions. We're a registered 501(c)(3); your gift is tax-deductible.

Why Give

A typical season costs us about $8,000.

Robot parts, FIRST registration fees, travel to qualifiers and championship, tools, outreach materials. We raise it from sponsors, fundraisers, and donations from people who believe in rural STEM.

Robot parts ~ $2,500
Registration & events ~ $1,500
Travel & lodging ~ $2,500
Tools & equipment ~ $1,000
Outreach materials ~ $500

01 / What Donations Buy

Concretely.

$ 25

A bag of fasteners

The unsung hero of every robot. Allen bolts, lock nuts, threadlocker. We go through hundreds in a season.

$ 75

3D printer filament

Enough PETG and PLA to print custom brackets, mounts, and prototypes for a month of build season.

$ 150

A motor or servo

FTC-legal motors run $80–$120; high-torque servos around $50. Robots eat motors during driver practice.

$ 300

Event registration

Covers a regional qualifier or league meet for the team. Includes pit space and judging session.

$ 500

A weekend at championship

Hotel rooms and meals for the team during a regional championship competition.

$ 1,000

An outreach season

School visits, library demos, community events, and FLL mentorship — for an entire calendar year.

Thank You

From a workshop in northwest Missouri.

If you've donated — past, present, or future — thank you. You're paying for the parts, the trips, the late nights, and the moment a student says "wait, I can actually build this."

If you have questions about a donation, want to make a gift another way, or need an acknowledgment letter, get in touch.
