Home / Outreach

Robotics shouldn't be a city-only opportunity.

Rural communities don't have fewer curious kids — they have fewer chances. We try to close that gap, one demo and one mentored team at a time.

Why Outreach

Pay it forward.

FIRST teams are scored at competitions on more than just robot performance. The judges' awards — Inspire, Connect, Motivate — recognize teams that grow their community. We take that seriously, because growing a robotics community in a town of two thousand people is not a side quest. It's the whole point.

Below: what we typically do across a year. If you run a school, library, or community group in our region and want a demo, get in touch.

01 / What We Do

Outreach activities across the year.

№ 01

FLL mentorship

Our older students mentor FIRST LEGO League teams at local elementary schools. We help them with strategy, programming, and judging prep.

Ongoing · Sept – May
№ 02

Library robot demos

Free public demos at regional libraries, often during the off-season. Kids drive the robot. Adults ask better questions than you'd expect.

Summer · Several events
№ 03

School STEM days

We bring the robot, a few demos, and our notebook to elementary and middle schools across northwest Missouri. The goal: plant seeds.

Fall · Multiple schools
№ 04

Community fair & parade

Local fairs, parades, and town events. The robot scores points. The team scores conversations with future students and sponsors.

Year-round
№ 05

Open shop nights

Periodic open-house nights when our build space opens to anyone who wants to drop in — see the robot, ask questions, learn what FTC actually is.

Periodic
№ 06

Rookie team support

We share resources, parts, and mentorship hours with new FTC teams in our region. A rising tide and all that.

As needed
