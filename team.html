Home / The Team

A dozen students, one workshop.

N.E.R.D.S stands for "Nerds Engineering Robots Diligently & Successfully" — a name we wrote when we were twelve and have been too stubborn to change.

Mission

Why we do this.

Northwest Missouri isn't a robotics hub. The nearest engineering school is hours away. Most of our students have never touched CAD software or written a line of Java before joining. That's exactly the point.

FTC gives us a problem each year — a real, physical, scoreable problem with rules and a clock — and asks us to solve it as a team. We use it as a vehicle for learning everything from mechanical design to public speaking. Some of us will become engineers. Some won't. All of us will leave knowing how to break a hard problem into smaller ones.

What we believe.

Mentors mentor — they don't build. Mistakes are how you learn. Documentation matters. Show your work. Help other teams. Lose graciously, win quietly, then go fix the autonomous routine.
